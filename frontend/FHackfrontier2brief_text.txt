1.1 Elevator Pitch  
MindDuel is an on -chain PvP skill game on Solana where players must answer trivia 
questions to earn the right to place a piece on a dynamic, ever -evolving Tic Tac Toe 
board — with real money on the line.  
 
Traditional Tic Tac Toe is deterministic and solved. MindDuel reinvents it by layering three new 
dimensions: knowledge (trivia gates every move), strategy (board mechanics that shift, scale, 
and surprise), and real stakes (trustless on -chain wagering with SOL/USDC escrow). The result 
is a game that is instantly familiar yet endlessly deep.  
 
1.2 The Problem  
Web3 gaming suffers from a fundamental credibility problem:  
• Games with token rewards attract mercenary players who leave once incentives dry up.  
• Most on -chain games use blockchain as a gimmick — the game itself would work fine as 
a Web2 app.  
• Existing casual games (Tic Tac Toe, Chess) have no native monetization or ownership 
layer.  
• PvP betting requires trust in a third -party escrow or centralized server — a single point of 
failure.  
 
1.3 The Solution  
MindDuel is genuinely on -chain by design:  
• Every move, trivia response, and game outcome is recorded on Solana — fully verifiable 
and replayable.  
• Escrow is trustless via Anchor smart contract — no admin keys, no rug risk.  
• The game loop is intrinsically fun without crypto — Web3 adds ownership and stakes, 
not substitutes for gameplay.  
• Hint economy creates a micro -transaction layer that is organic and user -driven, not 
forced.

2.1 The Fundamental Loop  
Trivia Gate → Correct Answer → Place Piece → Board Evolves → Repeat  
 
Every turn in MindDuel follows a strict sequence:  
1. Player's turn begins. A trivia question is fetched (category locked per match).  
2. Player sees the question and a countdown timer (configurable: 10 –30 seconds).  
3. Player may optionally purchase a Hint before answering (see Section 3.2).  
4. Player submits answer. A commit -reveal scheme on -chain verifies correctness without 
revealing the answer to the opponent.  
5. Correct answer: Player places piece on any valid board cell. Move recorded on -chain.  
6. Wrong answer or timeout: Turn is forfeited. Opponent gets a consecutive bonus turn.  
7. Board state evolves (depending on game mode). Win condition checked.  
 
2.2 Game Modes  
 
Mode 1 — Classic Duel (3x3)  
The entry point. Standard Tic Tac Toe board, but every move is gated by a trivia question. Win 
condition: 3 in a row. Perfect for new players and the fastest path to a completed match. This is 
the MVP mode and will be the first to reach testnet.  
 
Mode 2 — Shifting Board  
Every 3 rounds, a random row or column shifts one position — pieces shift with it.  
Shifts are deterministic (seeded from on -chain randomness via slot hash) — both players see 
the shift happen simultaneously. A shift can:  
• Move a player's winning row off -alignment at the last second.  
• Accidentally create a new winning line for the opponent.  
• Block a cell that was previously open.  
Strategy tip: Count rounds. If you are 2 rounds away from a shift, factor it into your move 
selection. The shift direction (left/right for rows, up/down for columns) is revealed 1 round in 
advance as a warning — adding another information layer to exploit.  
 
Mode 3 — Scale Up

by your opponent's last move. Win a sub -board to claim it; win 3 sub -boards in the meta -grid to 
win the match. Estimated match duration: 20 –40 minutes. This mode  will be scoped for post -
hackathon if time does not permit during the 18 -day sprint.

3.1 Stake & Wager System  
MindDuel supports two play modes: Free Play (no money at risk, leaderboard points only) and 
Stake Mode (real SOL/USDC on the line).  
 
In Stake Mode, both players deposit equal amounts into an Anchor -managed escrow 
before the match begins. The winner claims the full pot. The protocol takes a 2.5% 
platform fee.  
 
Stake Tiers  
Tier Wager Range  Typical Match  
Casual  0.01 – 0.1 SOL  Quick 3x3 Classic  
Challenger  0.1 – 1 SOL  Shifting Board / Scale Up  
High Stakes  1 SOL+ / Custom USDC  Blitz or arranged matches  
 
Draw Resolution  
If the board fills without a winner (possible in 3x3), the smart contract splits the pot 50/50, with 
the platform fee still deducted from the total. In Scale Up mode, a draw triggers one final 
Sudden Death Question — first to answer correctly wins the full  pot. 
 
3.2 Hint System  
The Hint system is the micro -economy at the heart of MindDuel. Players can purchase hints 
during their trivia turn using SOL. Hints are priced in micro -SOL and go directly into the protocol 
treasury (separate from the match escrow).

Hint purchases are instant, on -chain micro -transactions — made possible by 
Solana's sub -cent fees. In a Web2 game, these fees would make micro -transactions 
uneconomical.  
 
All hint revenue is logged on -chain and publicly verifiable. 80% goes to the protocol treasury; 
20% is reserved for the prize pool of weekly community tournaments.  
 
3.3 NFT Achievements & Match Records  
 
Streak Badges  
Win 3, 5, or 10 matches in a row → automatically mint a Streak Badge NFT (Metaplex). Badges 
are soulbound (non -transferable) — they represent proven skill, not purchasable status.  
 
Epic Game NFTs  
The smart contract tracks a "drama score" per match — calculated from: number of board shifts, 
consecutive correct answers, comebacks from a losing position, and overtime events. Matches 
above a drama threshold are flagged as Epic Games and can be optional ly minted as NFTs. 
The NFT stores the full move history, making it a permanent, playable replay stored on -chain.  
 
Leaderboard & Ranked Points  
Every match awards ranked points regardless of stake mode. Points determine global 
leaderboard placement. Top 10 wallets each week receive a share of the hint treasury's 
community prize pool — creating a sustainable, organic reward loop that does not depen d on 
token inflation.

4.1 System Overview  
MindDuel is composed of four layers: the Solana smart contract (on -chain truth), the frontend 
client (player interface), the trivia service (off -chain question bank with on -chain verification), 
and the indexer (match history and leaderboard).

4.2 Smart Contract Design  
 
Game Account Structure  
Each match creates a GameAccount PDA storing: player pubkeys, escrow amount, 
board state, turn counter, hint ledger, drama score, and win/draw flag.  
 
Trivia Commit -Reveal  
To prevent cheating (e.g., a player reading the answer from the transaction before submitting), 
MindDuel uses a two -phase commit -reveal scheme:  
8. Backend hashes the correct answer: hash = keccak256(answer + salt).  
9. Hash is stored in the GameAccount before the question is shown to the player.  
10. Player submits their answer on -chain.  
11. Backend reveals the salt; the contract computes the hash and compares. Match = 
correct.  
This guarantees that even with full on -chain transparency, no player can pre -read the answer.  
 
Escrow Safety

4.4 On -Chain Randomness for Board Shifts  
Board shifts in Shifting Board mode use Solana's recent slot hash as a pseudo -random seed. 
The shift direction and affected row/column are computed deterministically from: 
keccak256(slot_hash + game_id + round_number). Both players can independently verify  the 
shift is not manipulated, since slot hashes are public and immutable.

5.1 Onboarding Flow  
MindDuel is designed for the 0 -to-playing experience in under 60 seconds:  
12. Land on mindduel.app. Connect Phantom wallet (one click).  
13. Choose: Free Play or Stake Mode.  
14. Select game mode (Classic recommended for first -timers).  
15. Choose trivia category (General Knowledge, Crypto, Science, Pop Culture, History).  
16. Share game link with opponent OR get matched via public lobby.  
17. Both players confirm and (in Stake Mode) deposit. Match begins.  
 
No signup. No email. No KYC. Wallet = identity. This is the Web3 onboarding 
experience it should be.  
 
5.2 In -Game Screen Layout  
The game screen is divided into three zones:  
• Top bar: Player addresses (truncated), wager amount, round counter, shift countdown 
(Shifting Board mode).  
• Center: The board — animated, responsive, with visual feedback for each piece 
placement.  
• Bottom: Trivia zone — question, answer choices, timer bar, Hint buttons.  
 
Board shifts are animated (Framer Motion slide transition) so players can clearly see what 
moved. A shift warning appears 1 round in advance, giving players time to strategize.  
 
5.3 Async Play  
Players do not need to be online simultaneously. Each turn has a configurable timeout (default: 
24 hours for Standard modes, 5 minutes for Blitz). If a player times out without answering, their 
turn is forfeited and a timeout is recorded on -chain. Three co nsecutive timeouts = forfeit match, 
opponent claims the pot.  
 
5.4 Match Replay  
After every match, both players can access a full animated replay of the game — every 
question, every answer, every board shift, reconstructed from on -chain data. Epic Game NFT 
holders can share a public replay link.
